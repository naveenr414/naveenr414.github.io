About

Hello! I'm a first year PhD student in the School of Computer Science at Carnegie Mellon University, funded by an NSF Fellowship. I previously obtained my MPhil in Advanced Computer Science on a Churchill Scholarship, where I worked with Mateja Jamnik to understand explainability through concept learning models. I received my BS in Computer Science and Math from the University of Maryland, during which I worked with John Dickerson on fairness in rideshare, and with Jordan Boyd-Graber on entity linking algorithms. During my summers, I worked at MIT Lincoln Labs and Facebook.

My research focuses on improving machine learning for healthcare, with a particular focus on decision making. This includes work on uncertainty in human-AI teaming [AIES'23], learning-to-defer algorithms [NeurIPS'21 WHMD], and my current work on explainable AI. I'm interested in tackling the following questions:

  1. How can we develop effective explainability method to assist practioners in real-world healthcare scenarios
  2. How can we develop algorithms to tackle time series data that reflects the realities of healthcare datasets, such as noise or missing data
  3. How can we overcome the limitations of machine learning algorithms when applied to healthcare, such as the need for robustness
If you want to chat about any of these topics, or just about research in general, feel free to reach out and contact me!
