About

Hello! I'm a PhD student in the Machine Learning Department at Carnegie Mellon University, advised by Fei Fang, and funded by an NSF Fellowship. My research focuses on human feedback in algorithmic decision-making, and our work has been deployed in domains such as food sustainability and behavioral health. Recently, I've been thinking about:

  • Evaluation: How can we best incorporate human preferences when evaluating AI systems?
  • Explainability: How can we design explainability methods that complement human decision-makers?
    (Concept Locality, Concepet Uncertainty, Inter-Concept Relationships)
  • Choice: How can we design mechanisms that allow for participant choice while maximizing overall welfare?
    (Patient-Provider Matching, Food Rescue Notifications)
I previously obtained my MPhil in Advanced Computer Science, where I worked with Mateja Jamnik to understand explainability through concept learning models. I received my BS in Computer Science and Math from the University of Maryland, during which I worked with John Dickerson on fairness in rideshare, and with Jordan Boyd-Graber on entity linking algorithms.

If you want to chat about any of these topics, or just about research in general, feel free to reach out!
